Compliance

Responsible data use should be part of the list from day one.

Marketing contact data must be handled carefully. This page explains the practical standards we build around: relevance, transparency, opt-out respect, and data minimization.

Principles

Our working standard for contact data projects.

This website does not provide legal advice. It gives practical operating principles that help teams avoid sloppy or misleading outreach practices.

Relevant targeting

Lists should match a legitimate business audience instead of broad, unrelated scraping.

Minimal fields

Collect only the fields needed for the campaign and avoid unnecessary personal information.

Opt-out readiness

Campaign teams should maintain unsubscribe and suppression workflows before outreach begins.

Data hygiene

Outdated, duplicate, incomplete, or questionable records should be cleaned or separated.

What clients should prepare

Use contact data carefully after delivery.

  • Confirm your outreach purpose and audience fit before sending campaigns.
  • Include clear sender information and a practical opt-out method in marketing messages.
  • Honor unsubscribe, do-not-contact, and suppression requests promptly.
  • Do not upload lists into tools that violate your platform terms or privacy obligations.
  • Review applicable laws and platform requirements for your market before sending outreach.

Important note

LeadLedger Data is positioned as a responsible marketing data support service. Any real business using this site should have its privacy policy, terms, opt-out process, and legal requirements reviewed by a qualified professional before collecting or selling contact data.
